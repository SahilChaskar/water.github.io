Freshwater Withdrawals

Freshwater withdrawals refer to the amount of freshwater taken from its source, such as rivers, lakes, or groundwater, for various purposes, including irrigation, industrial processes, and domestic use. These withdrawals have a significant impact on water resources.

Freshwater Consumption

Freshwater consumption represents the portion of freshwater withdrawals that is consumed and not returned to the water source. High levels of freshwater consumption can lead to water scarcity in certain regions.

World Water Use

World water use encompasses the global distribution and consumption of freshwater resources. Changes in water use patterns, influenced by factors such as population growth and industrial development, have significant implications for the availability and sustainability of water resources worldwide.
